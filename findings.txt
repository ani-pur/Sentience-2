FINDING: Zero Churn/Advocacy Rates for Select Brands
=====================================================

Brands affected: Alibaba, DeepSeek, Google, Microsoft

OBSERVATION
-----------
The churn rate and advocacy rate metrics display as 0.0% for the above brands
while showing non-zero values for OpenAI and Anthropic.

ROOT CAUSE
----------
This is a dataset issue, not a frontend bug. The churn and advocacy signals are
detected via keyword matching against Reddit post text. The keyword lists were
designed around consumer-facing AI products:

  Churn keywords:    "switching to", "cancelled", "unsubscribed", "leaving", etc.
  Advocacy keywords: "recommend", "best ai", "love this", "amazing", "game changer", etc.

Reddit discussions about OpenAI and Anthropic are driven heavily by paying
consumers who use these phrases naturally when venting or praising. Reddit
discussions about Alibaba (Qwen), DeepSeek, Google (Gemini), and Microsoft
(Copilot) tend to be more news-oriented, technical, or research-focused. These
posts rarely contain the exact consumer-style phrases the pipeline looks for,
resulting in genuinely zero signals in the aggregated data.

IMPLICATION FOR PROJECTIONS
----------------------------
Because churn rate and advocacy rate are two of the six features fed into the
LSTM, brands with zero signals in both fields are effectively missing two input
dimensions. The LSTM still produces projections but relies more heavily on
weighted sentiment score, post volume, emotion intensity, and comment alignment
for those brands. Projections for Alibaba, DeepSeek, Google, and Microsoft may
therefore be less sensitive to loyalty-related shifts than projections for
OpenAI and Anthropic.

OPTIONS TO ADDRESS
------------------
1. Accept as real data. The absence of consumer churn/advocacy language is itself
   a meaningful signal about how each brand is discussed online.

2. Expand the keyword lists to include more technical and enterprise language
   (e.g. advocacy: "impressive", "underrated", "solid"; churn: "overrated",
   "not worth it", "going back"). This would require re-running the features
   and aggregate pipeline stages against MongoDB.


FINDING: Negative Sentiment Skew Due to Platform Selection Bias
================================================================

OBSERVATION
-----------
Across all tracked brands, our Composite Sentiment Scores trend lower than what
industry-standard multi-platform analyses typically report. Bearish sentiment
drivers appear disproportionately represented relative to bullish ones, and
overall sentiment distributions lean negative even for brands with broadly
favorable public perception.

ROOT CAUSE - Platform Dynamics
-------------------------------
Our dataset is sourced exclusively from Reddit. Reddit's platform dynamics
introduce a well-documented negativity bias that must be accounted for when
interpreting results:

  1. HIGHER BARRIER TO ENTRY: Reddit requires users to create an account, join
     specific subreddits, and navigate community norms before posting. This
     self-selection means that the users who do post tend to be more motivated
     - and dissatisfaction is a stronger motivator than satisfaction. Users with
     positive experiences are less likely to take the extra effort to post about
     them, whereas frustrated users are actively incentivized to vent.

  2. NEGATIVITY AMPLIFICATION: Reddit's upvote/downvote system and comment
     threading tend to surface contentious and critical threads over neutral or
     positive ones. Complaint posts generate more engagement (comments, upvotes)
     and therefore more visibility in the dataset.

  3. ABSENCE OF TWITTER/X DATA: Twitter/X has a significantly lower barrier to
     posting - a quick tweet requires minimal effort compared to a Reddit post.
     This lower friction captures a wider spectrum of casual, positive, and
     reactionary sentiment that our pipeline does not have access to. We were
     unable to incorporate Twitter/X data due to API access limitations. Had
     Twitter data been included, we would expect a more balanced sentiment
     distribution with stronger bullish representation, as positive micro-
     reactions ("love this update", "solid W") are far more common on Twitter
     than on Reddit.

IMPACT ON RESULTS
-----------------
  - Composite Sentiment Scores may read 5-15 points lower than a multi-platform
    benchmark would suggest.
  - Bearish sentiment drivers are overrepresented in the news feed relative to
    actual public opinion.
  - LSTM projections inherit this skew - the model learns from Reddit-specific
    discourse patterns, which means forecasted sentiment trends are calibrated
    to Reddit's negativity baseline rather than the broader internet.

CONCLUSION
----------
This is a known and expected artifact of single-platform sourcing. The relative
trends (brand-to-brand comparisons, week-over-week movement) remain valid and
analytically useful because the bias is consistent across all brands and time
periods. Absolute sentiment scores, however, should be interpreted as
Reddit-specific rather than representative of total public sentiment. Future
iterations incorporating Twitter/X, news article NLP, or Bluesky data would
yield a more balanced and generalizable sentiment profile.
